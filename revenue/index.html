Questions

The honest answers

What is the AI Revenue Accelerator?

A small, application-only room where women founders build a working AI revenue engine on their actual business, not a demo. Week by week you set up the system that finds your buyers, writes outreach in your voice, follows up before warm leads go cold, and briefs you for the call that closes. You leave with the engine running and a pipeline you can see, not a folder of notes.

Who is it for?

Women founders already earning who can feel the ceiling: the offer works, the audience is there, but every sale still starts from scratch because there's no system behind it. If AI has made you tidier but not richer, this was built for you.

Do I need to be technical?

No, and the room isn't built for engineers. Most AI advice is either a basic prompting demo or written for developers, so founders assume the useful part is out of reach. It isn't. The engine runs with AI doing the heavy lifting and you staying in command, and if you can explain your business, your buyer, and your offer, you can build it. What you need isn't code, it's a business that's earning and the will to do the deep work.

What will I actually leave with?

Four things, running before the room ends: your likeliest buyers identified by AI that reads your market, outreach and follow-up going out in your voice on time every time, a pipeline that fills so Monday starts with meetings booked instead of a blank page, and the CRO habits that keep it full. It's all built on your context, and it's yours for good. The engine doesn't stop when the room does, it compounds.

How is this different from the AI courses I've already bought?

A course hands you information and wishes you luck. Here you don't watch modules about outreach, you leave with outreach running, on your business, in your voice, aimed at your buyers. Every piece is battle-tested because I run my own company on it, and every mistake it saves you from is one I paid for first. And because you build it yourself, alongside us, you can run it, fix it, and grow it without me.

Why is it an application?

Because the quality of the room is very important to Ella. Small rooms only work when every founder in them has real revenue, a real pipeline to build, and the intent to build it, so I filter for that instead of selling seats. I read every application myself. It's a few honest answers about your business, not an essay, and it costs you nothing but the truth.

How do I get in?

Apply and tell me two things: what you're building, and the one revenue task you keep not getting to. That second answer tells me more than any pitch deck, it's usually the first thing the engine takes off your plate. I reply within 48 hours.

What if I'm not ready for the paid room?

Then don't apply yet. Ready was never a feeling, it's a business with revenue to grow, so go grow it. Keep pointing your AI at sales instead of admin, and when you've got a pipeline worth systemizing, the application's here.
