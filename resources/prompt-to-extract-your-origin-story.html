I’ve watched thousands of founders try to sell something.

The ones who convert aren’t the ones with the best product. They’re the ones who can tell you why they started. The real reason. Not the polished version. The actual moment when they looked around and said “this is broken and I’m going to fix it.”

Your origin story - is the only narrative that actually works.

But here’s the problem: Most people don’t know how to extract theirs.

They know it happened. They can feel it. But the second they sit down to write it or say it out loud, it becomes corporate-speak. Mission statements. Value propositions. The kiss of death.

What they need is a framework that pulls the truth out without fluffing it.

Why Your Origin Story Matters More Than Your Business Plan

Your business exists because something broke you or you found your superpower.

Maybe you watched an industry that didn’t serve women. Maybe you got tired of being the bottleneck in your own company. Maybe you realized that everyone in the room was men and that couldn’t be an accident.

That moment- that’s your moat. That’s what makes you unforgettable.

But most founders bury it. They think it’s not “professional” to say “I got angry” or “I realized I was being left behind” or “I watched my male co-founder do in two months what I thought would take a year.”

So they write some generic mission statement instead.

And their ideal customer scrolls past.

The women who are going to follow you aren’t following you because of your product. They’re following you because your story mirrors theirs. Because you figured something out they’re still wrestling with. Because you’re proof that it’s possible.

Your origin story is the permission slip they didn’t know they needed.

The Origin Story Extractor Prompt

I built a framework that pulls your real story out and maps it onto narrative structure that actually converts.

Here’s how it works:

You feed it:

  • What you do (specific)

  • Your core offer

  • Who you serve + their actual problems

  • Why you started (the real reason)

It asks you clarifying questions that force the truth out. Not “what is your mission?” but “what made you angry enough to start something?”

It maps your journey onto classic archetypes (Guide, Rebel, Hero, Sage) so you understand what you’re communicating and why it lands.

It gives you your narrative spine:

  • The problem or tension that broke you

  • The turning point

  • The transformation you went through

  • What you now help others do

Then it tells you exactly how to use that story in your messaging: your homepage, your LinkedIn, your emails, your sales calls.

(Everyone wants to skip straight to building agents. Your story is why they won’t scroll past you when you do.)

How to Set This Up in Claude (Right Now)

Copy this. Paste into Claude. Fill in your details. 

ROLE: You are Donald Miller, founder of StoryBrand and author of Building a Story Brand.

CONTEXT: I want to turn my business story into a powerful narrative that connects deeply with my audience and positions my offer in a way that feels meaningful, memorable, and clear.

Business Details:
- What I do: [INSERT BUSINESS DESCRIPTION]
- Core offer: [INSERT MAIN SERVICE/PRODUCT]
- Ideal customer: [INSERT WHO I SERVE + THEIR MAIN CHALLENGES]
- Why I started (if relevant): [INSERT OR INFER]

REQUEST: Before you begin, ask any clarifying questions needed to fully understand my story, motivation, and audience.

Then create a narrative breakdown with:

1. Archetype & Story Structure
- Map my journey to a classic archetype or story (Hero's Journey, Guide, Rebel, Sage, etc.)
- Explain why this archetype fits and what it reveals about my positioning

2. My Story (Simplified Narrative)
- Break my journey into:
  - The problem or tension
  - The turning point
  - The transformation
  - What I now help others do

3. The Deeper Connection
- Reveal the hidden connection between my story and my audience's story
- Show how my journey mirrors or solves what they are going through
- Make this insight feel surprising and powerful

4. Brand Positioning Shift
- Reframe my business using this narrative
- Clarify:
  - Who the hero is (customer)
  - What problem they face
  - How I guide them
  - What success looks like

5. How to Communicate This Online
- Give specific, practical ways to express this story across:
  - Website homepage
  - Social media content
  - About page
  - Sales messaging
- Include example lines, hooks, or phrases I can use

REQUIREMENTS:
- Make this feel insightful and emotionally intelligent, not generic branding advice
- Focus on clarity + resonance, not complexity
- Avoid fluff—every insight should help me communicate better
- Avoid double dashes
- Use the Humanizer skill
- Write in a way that helps me immediately apply this to my messaging


REQUEST: Ask clarifying questions. Then map my journey to a narrative archetype (Guide, Rebel, Hero, Sage). Break my story into: problem → turning point → transformation → what I help others do. Show how my journey mirrors my audience's. Give me specific lines I can use right now in my messaging. Rate your work I want to get to a 9/10 if you're not there. Self verify your work by testing it end to end. Do not return control to me until you have met the requirements and it is working as expected.

That’s literally it.

30 minutes later, you have your narrative spine. Your archetype. Your hooks. Done.

No fluff. No “align your values with your mission statement.” Just: here’s why people actually care about what you do.

The ones who convert aren’t the ones with the best pitch deck. They’re the ones whose story is so true it leaves no room for doubt.
