# Email 2 · Day 2 · Did you run the prompt?

**Subject:** Did you run it?
**Preheader:** Two minutes today beats a course you never finish.

---

Quick one. Two days ago you got your reading and one working prompt.

If you ran it: good. You just did the whole method in miniature. Context in, judgment out, hours back.

If you did not, it is usually one of these:

**"It gave me something generic."** That is a context problem, not a you problem. Add three sentences before the prompt: who you are, who you serve, and one example of your writing or your calendar. Generic in, generic out. Specific in, and it suddenly sounds like you hired someone.

Here is one more, free. This one is the fastest confidence win I know:

**The decline-nicely prompt**
Paste with any request you have been sitting on because saying no feels heavy.

> Draft a two-line reply declining this warmly and plainly, in my voice. No apology spiral, no fake busyness, no "maybe later" if I mean no. Offer one genuinely useful alternative if one exists, otherwise just close it kindly.

You have been composing that email in your head for a week. Let the assistant take the first pass.

Warmly,
Ella
